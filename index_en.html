  • Bio

    A little about me

    Anna Dzhambuliyevna Chakvetadze is a Russian professional tennis player.
    She was born on March 5, 1987 in Moscow. Anna's father is from Sochi, he's a businessman, and her mother Natalia is from Volgograd, she's a housewife.

    She began to play tennis when she was 8 years old, her first coach was Victor Pavlov. She trained in the child international tennis academy "Valery". She learned from the American Chris Evert at the academy.



    RESULTS

    • Champion of Russia, 2001 (under 16 years), 2002 (under 18 years).
    • Winner of the International youth games of CIS, Baltic states, and regions of Russia under the patronage of the IOC (2002)
    • Winner of the RTT Cup, 2002 (under 18 years).
    • Numerous winner of the "European cup" as a member of Russia youth national team (16-18 years).
    • In juniors' tennis: winner of six junior ITF tournaments, 2nd category (once), 3rd category (once), and 4th category (four times).
    • Finalist of Wimbledon junior champion, 2003.
    • In professional tour since 2003.

    Anna Chakvetadze's star began to rise on the tennis sky in 2004 when in her first appearance at the Grand Slams she sensationally defeated Anastasia Myskina in the second round of US Open with a score of 7-6 (3) 6-3.


    On October 15, 2006, she became the winner of the XVIIth international tennis tournament "Kremlin Cup".
